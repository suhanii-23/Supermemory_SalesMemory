Re: Oracle — closed won retro

Seven months, largest deal I've run. A few things worth remembering for future large enterprise deals with similarly rigorous internal processes:

1. When a buyer tells you upfront the review will be extensive, believe them and plan for it — don't discount it as a generic caution. Padma was accurate on every timeline estimate she gave throughout.
2. Keeping the security review track alive during the budget freeze meant we lost zero time once the freeze lifted — huge in hindsight, could have added another month or two if we'd let that track go cold too.
3. Competitive positioning (Gong) worked because we validated the existing tool rather than attacking it. Would have backfired with Robert specifically.
4. Pricing risk transparently (the termination-for-convenience premium) rather than either refusing or absorbing it silently gave procurement something concrete to model, and they appreciated having a real choice.
5. Padma explicitly said afterward that the rigor of the process, while frustrating in the moment, is *why* she trusts the deal now. Worth remembering: slow and thorough isn't always a bad sign with sophisticated enterprise buyers, sometimes it's the buyer's own risk management working as intended.
